Aloo Paratha

Aloo Paratha is a popular and delicious Indian flatbread stuffed with spiced mashed potatoes.Despite being a comfort food, Aloo Paratha can be made healthier by using whole wheat flour for the outer layer and incorporating a balanced mix of spices.

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, salt, and oil (if using). Gradually add water and knead to form a soft and smooth dough.
  2. In another bowl, mix the boiled and mashed potatoes with finely chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, chopped coriander leaves, and salt.
  3. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the rolled-out dough.
  4. Bring the edges of the dough together to cover the filling and seal it. Press gently to flatten.
  5. Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium heat. Place the rolled paratha on the hot tawa.
  6. Cook until bubbles appear on the surface. Flip the paratha and cook the other side, applying oil or ghee around the edges.
  7. Continue flipping and cooking until both sides are golden brown and well-cooked.
  8. Remove from the tawa and serve the hot Aloo Parathas with yogurt, pickles, or any desired accompaniment.

Ingredients:

  1. 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  2. Water (as needed for kneading)
  3. 3 to 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  4. 1 small onion, finely chopped
  5. 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  6. 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  7. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  8. 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  9. 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  10. 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  11. Fresh coriander leaves, choppedr
